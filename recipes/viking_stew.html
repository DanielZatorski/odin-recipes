Viking Stew (Skause)


Skause (sometimes spelled skause or skálsa) is a hearty Viking stew rooted in the culinary traditions of medieval Scandinavia. This dish was a staple for Norse warriors and seafarers, known for its nourishing ingredients and ease of preparation over an open fire. Often made with available root vegetables, dried legumes, and preserved meats or fish, Skause is a rustic, adaptable stew meant to sustain through cold weather and long voyages.

While there is no single historical recipe for Skause, this modern interpretation stays true to its Viking spirit—simple, filling, and rich with flavor. It’s perfect for a cozy winter meal or as part of a medieval or Norse-themed gathering.

Serve with traditional flatbread or dark rye for an authentic Viking experience.


Skause – Ingredients

Makes 4–6 servings

  • 500 g (1 lb) lamb or beef, diced (or use smoked meat for authenticity)
  • 2 tbsp animal fat or oil (like lard or butter)
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 2–3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 3 carrots, peeled and sliced
  • 2 parsnips or turnips, chopped
  • 3–4 potatoes, diced
  • 100 g (½ cup) dried peas or lentils (soaked for 1 hour)
  • 1.5 liters (6 cups) water or broth (meat or vegetable)
  • 1 tsp dried thyme or wild herbs (optional)
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Procedure

  1. Brown the meat

    In a large pot or Dutch oven, heat the fat or oil over medium heat. Add the diced meat and cook until browned on all sides.

  2. Sauté the aromatics

    Add the chopped onion and garlic. Sauté for 3–5 minutes until softened and fragrant.

  3. Add vegetables and legumes

    Stir in the carrots, parsnips, potatoes, and soaked peas or lentils. Cook for another 2–3 minutes.

  4. Add liquid and season

    Pour in the water or broth. Add thyme (if using), salt, and pepper. Stir well and bring to a boil.

  5. Simmer

    Reduce heat to low, cover, and let simmer for 1.5 to 2 hours, stirring occasionally, until meat is tender and stew has thickened.

  6. Adjust seasoning

    Taste and adjust salt and pepper as needed before serving.

  7. Serve

    Ladle hot into bowls and serve with flatbread or rye. Garnish with herbs if desired.
