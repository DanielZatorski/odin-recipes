Viking Flatbread (Hviti Brauð)


Hvít Brauð, literally meaning “white bread” in Icelandic, is a soft, slightly sweet, and fluffy loaf that's a staple in Icelandic households. Made with simple ingredients—usually wheat flour, milk or water, sugar, yeast, and a bit of salt—it’s known for its tender crumb and golden-brown crust. The dough is typically enriched with milk and sometimes a touch of butter or oil, giving it a rich and comforting flavor.

This bread is often baked in a rectangular loaf pan, resulting in a tall, uniform shape perfect for slicing. It’s commonly enjoyed with butter, cheese, or jam, and is especially popular at breakfast or during afternoon coffee breaks (kaffitími). Some Icelanders associate it with childhood and comfort, evoking memories of home-cooked meals and family gatherings.

While not as rustic or hearty as rye-based Icelandic breads like rúgbrauð, Hvít Brauð offers a soft and mild contrast, making it a versatile choice for everyday use.


Hvít Brauð – Ingredients

For 1 large loaf (or 2 small loaves):

  • 500 g (4 cups) all-purpose flour
  • 300 ml (1 ¼ cups) warm milk (can substitute part or all with warm water)
  • 2 tbsp sugar
  • 2 tbsp butter (softened or melted)
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 2 ¼ tsp (1 packet or 7 g) active dry yeast
  • Optional: 1 egg (for egg wash, gives a shiny crust)

Procedure

  1. Make the dough

    In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour and salt. Add the yeast mixture and melted butter. Mix until a dough forms.

  2. Knead the dough

    Place the dough in a lightly greased bowl, cover it with a clean towel or plastic wrap, and let it rise in a warm place for about 1 hour, or until doubled in size.

  3. First dough rise

    Shape the dough into a ball, place it in a lightly oiled bowl, cover with a clean towel or plastic wrap, and let rise in a warm place for 60–90 minutes, or until doubled in size

  4. Shape and Second Rise

    Punch down the dough, shape into a loaf (or two smaller loaves), and place in a greased loaf pan (or on a parchment-lined baking sheet if free-form). Cover and let rise for again for about 30 to 45 minutes until puffy.

  5. Preheat the oven

    Preheat to 180°C (350°F).

  6. Optional Egg Wash and Topping

    Brush the top with a beaten egg and sprinkle with seeds if desired.

  7. Bake

    Bake for 30–40 minutes (25–30 if making two smaller loaves), until golden brown and it sounds hollow when tapped on the bottom. Internal temp should be around 90°C (195°F).

  8. Cool

    Let cool in the pan for 10 minutes, then remove and cool on a rack.
